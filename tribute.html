Tribute to Jackie Robinson

Jack Roosevelt Robinson (January 31, 1919 â€“ October 24, 1972) was an American professional baseball player. He became the first African American to play in Major League Baseball (MLB) in the modern era. Robinson broke the baseball color line when he made his debut with the Brooklyn Dodgers on April 15, 1947.

Robinson's courage and exceptional skills on the field paved the way for the integration of professional sports in the United States. He faced significant adversity and racism throughout his career but persevered, becoming a symbol of hope and inspiration.

Jackie Robinson Timeline

  • January 31, 1919
    Jackie Robinson is born in Cairo, Georgia.
  • 1942
    Robinson joins the United States Army during World War II.
  • April 15, 1947
    Robinson makes his Major League Baseball debut with the Brooklyn Dodgers, becoming the first African American to play in the modern era.
  • 1949
    Robinson wins the National League Most Valuable Player (MVP) award.
  • 1955
    Robinson helps the Brooklyn Dodgers win the World Series.
  • 1962
    Robinson is inducted into the Baseball Hall of Fame.
  • October 24, 1972
    Jackie Robinson passes away in Stamford, Connecticut.
